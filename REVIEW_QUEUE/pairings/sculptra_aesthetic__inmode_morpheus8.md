## Content Fields

### Clinical Rationale
Sculptra stimulates collagen through PLLA particles that trigger a gradual biologic response over months. Morpheus8 stimulates collagen through controlled radiofrequency thermal energy that triggers an immediate wound-healing response. These represent two different pathways to collagen stimulation -- biologic (Sculptra) and thermal (Morpheus8). Sculptra also provides volume restoration that Morpheus8 cannot achieve, while Morpheus8 provides skin tightening and texture improvement that Sculptra cannot achieve. Note: No published controlled trial data exists for this specific combination. The FDA Sculptra label states that safety and effectiveness in combination with other products and procedures have not been evaluated in controlled clinical trials.

### Timing Guidance
Can be same session or sequential. If sequential: Sculptra series first to build collagen foundation, then layer Morpheus8 for texture and tightening. Maintenance: Sculptra annually, Morpheus8 1-2x per year. Providers who use this combination in practice may vary on timing and sequencing.

### Patient Education Text
Tell the patient: "Sculptra works beneath the surface to gradually support collagen production -- it addresses the structural side. Morpheus8 uses radiofrequency energy to improve skin texture and firmness from a different angle. Your provider may suggest using both, depending on your individual concerns. This combination is used in clinical practice, but it has not been evaluated in controlled clinical trials, so your provider can discuss whether it makes sense for your situation."

### Staff Talking Points
"When a patient has both volume loss and skin quality concerns, this combination may address both from different angles. 'Sculptra supports collagen production gradually over months. RF microneedling improves texture, firmness, and fine lines through controlled thermal stimulation.' This combination is used in clinical practice but has not been evaluated in controlled trials. Present it as an option the provider may recommend based on the patient's individual needs. Let patients know results are gradual -- this is about supporting their skin's long-term health."

## Evidence

- **FDA Sculptra Aesthetic labeling:** Indicated for correction of shallow to deep nasolabial fold contour deficiencies and other facial wrinkles. "The safety and effectiveness of SCULPTRA Aesthetic used in combination with other products and procedures have not been evaluated in controlled clinical trials."
- **Expert consensus:** Expert consensus supports combining biostimulators with energy-based devices in clinical practice, based on the rationale that PLLA and RF microneedling stimulate collagen through different mechanisms.
- **Evidence Type: LOW (expert consensus only; no published combination data; FDA label notes combination use not systematically evaluated)**
