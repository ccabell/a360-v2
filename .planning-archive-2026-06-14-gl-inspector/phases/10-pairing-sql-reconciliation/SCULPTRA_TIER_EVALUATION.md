## Decision Framework

### Mechanism-Based Reasoning vs Evidence-Based Reasoning

The distinction that drives these verdicts:

**Neurotoxin + Sculptra:**
- Neuromodulation (blocks acetylcholine release at neuromuscular junction) operates entirely independently of PLLA collagen stimulation (triggers foreign body response leading to fibroblast activity)
- Zero mechanistic interaction — these are completely orthogonal biological processes at different tissue depths (intramuscular vs subdermal)
- The same argument that makes neurotoxin + HA filler a canonical/common pair (different mechanisms, different aging problems) applies here
- Key difference from canonical neurotoxin + HA pairs: those have published PubMed data (DOI 10.1097/DSS.0000000000000754); neurotoxin + Sculptra has expert consensus only
- Rubric threshold for common: 7-8 gates pass + 1+ source — these meet that with 7 PASS + expert consensus as the 1 source

**HA Filler + Sculptra:**
- Both are injectable products working in adjacent tissue planes on similar aging concerns (volume loss)
- Mechanism overlap is higher — both address volume restoration, though via different pathways
- Same-session complexity is greater because injecting two volumizing agents at nearby depths requires more clinical judgment
- Per Chris's feedback on rows 26-31: same-session language should be conditional → this is appropriate for the conditional tier where conditions must be explicitly stated
- No published combination data pushes toward conditional

**Sculptra + Energy Device:**
- Different modalities (injectable vs energy) but both stimulate collagen via different mechanisms
- Could potentially be common, but expert-consensus-only evidence without any published combination data is a weaker basis than neurotoxin + biostimulator
- The absence of the orthogonality argument (both are collagen stimulators, just via different pathways) makes the "why both?" case slightly weaker
- Conditional is appropriate: works under specific clinical sequencing conditions

## Gate 8 Analysis for Neurotoxin + Sculptra Pairs

| Gate 8 Status | Evidence Found |
|---------------|----------------|
| PARTIAL | Expert consensus supports neurotoxin + biostimulator combinations in clinical practice |
| Corpus query | No published controlled trial for the specific combination |
| FDA Sculptra label | "Safety and effectiveness of SCULPTRA Aesthetic used in combination with other products and procedures have not been evaluated in controlled clinical trials" |

**Why PARTIAL qualifies for common tier:**
- The rubric states: "common requires 1+ source" — expert consensus is a source
- The rubric states: "no corpus evidence = max conditional" — there IS corpus evidence (expert consensus); this is not a zero-evidence pair
- PARTIAL (not PASS) on Gate 8 means the evidence is there but not as strong as full PubMed data
- With 7 other gates at PASS, this meets the 7-8 gates + 1+ source threshold for common
