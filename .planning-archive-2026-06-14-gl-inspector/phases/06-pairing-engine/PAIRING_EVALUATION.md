### Batch 1: Neurotoxin x HA Filler (25 pairs) -- Priority 1

**Batch rationale:** Highest-value canonical candidates. Most common combination in aesthetics. Neurotoxin addresses dynamic wrinkles (muscle movement); HA filler addresses volume loss, static lines. Different mechanisms, different tissue layers.

**Category-level corpus evidence:** 90-woman dose-ranging study confirming combination superiority (episode b2b96f9e). Dr. Teri Fisher: "Botox immobilizes muscles, minimizing movement and allowing fillers to last longer." Tracy Mancuso full stacking protocol places neurotoxin + filler as the final layers. Multiple shows confirm same-session compatibility (neurotoxin first, then filler). France legal flag noted (Dr. Martschin, Ep 169).

**Category-level gate analysis (applies to all 25 pairs):**
- **G1 (Concern Overlap): PASS** -- Both categories address facial aging concerns (Fine Lines, Volume Loss, Skin Aging). Neurotoxins treat dynamic wrinkles; HA fillers treat static volume loss. Shared patient concern: facial aging/rejuvenation.
- **G2 (Mechanism Complementarity): PASS** -- Neurotoxin = neuromodulation (muscle relaxation). HA filler = volume restoration (gel scaffold). Completely different mechanisms at different tissue layers.
- **G3 (Limitation Coverage): PASS** -- Neurotoxin does_not_solve: "Cannot restore lost volume." HA filler directly addresses volume restoration. HA filler does_not_solve: "Cannot address dynamic wrinkles caused by muscle movement." Neurotoxin directly addresses this.
- **G4 (Timing): PASS** -- Same-session compatible (neurotoxin first, then filler). Multiple expert protocols confirm. France exception noted.
- **G5 (Safety): PASS** -- No known contraindications. Dose-ranging study confirms safety. Post-cannula 48-hour makeup restriction applies to filler component.
- **G6 (Commercial): PASS** -- Most common combination in medical aesthetics. AmSpa data: bundled practices see 25-40% higher annual spend. "Combination one, hands down."
- **G7 (Patient Clarity): PASS** -- "Botox relaxes the muscles that cause lines, but it can't restore volume that's been lost. Filler addresses a different problem." Clear, non-promotional.
- **G8 (Source Support): PASS** -- 20+ chunks across 8+ shows. 90-woman study. Multiple expert endorsements. Evidence strength 5/5.

**Tier determination for Botox specifically:** Botox is the best-documented neurotoxin. Botox + each HA filler gets the strongest evidence. Other neurotoxins (Dysport, Jeuveau, Daxxify, Xeomin) have equivalent clinical mechanism but less product-specific corpus evidence -- they inherit category-level evidence at the common tier.
