## Adjudication Decisions

### Phrases Included With Multiple-Concern Mapping

1. **"I always look tired"** — mapped to BOTH Brow Ptosis AND Tear Trough Hollowing. Decision: included in both. The phrase routes through both concerns because tired appearance is multi-mechanism.

2. **"My skin looks tired"** — mapped to Skin Dullness (specific symptom). Separate phrase "I always look tired" mapped to Tear Trough Hollowing (anatomical mechanism).

3. **"Look tired from brows"** — mapped exclusively to Brow Ptosis (brow-specific anatomical language). Not mapped to Tear Trough Hollowing.

### Phrases Considered But Rejected

1. **"I need Botox"** — treatment preference, not a concern. Rejected across all instances.
2. **"I want to look better overall"** — too vague to route. Rejected.
3. **"I don't want to look frozen"** — objection/fear, not a concern. Rejected.
4. **"I just want natural results"** — preference, not a concern. Rejected.
5. **"Hair thinning"** — outside aesthetic scope for this practice (no GL product for hair growth). Deferred.

### New Concern Candidates Deferred

The following patient phrases appeared in transcripts but were not added as new concerns. They need Chris review:

1. **Stretch Marks** — Multiple patients mentioned "stretch marks from pregnancy" or "after losing weight." No concern in DB. Candidate concern: "Stretch Marks" (category: skin_quality). Currently no product in GL addresses this directly.
2. **Cellulite (standalone)** — Patients distinguished cellulite from body fat ("I don't want to lose weight, I want to fix the dimples"). Currently routed to Buttock Appearance. May warrant its own concern.
3. **Eye Bags (lower lid fat pads)** — "Puffy bags under my eyes" is anatomically distinct from tear trough hollowing. Currently both route to Tear Trough Hollowing. May need disambiguation.
