**## Tier Definitions**
Define each tier with: description, evidence requirement, content depth, DB emission rule. Per D-12:
- `canonical`: Universally recommended; all 8 gates pass; strong corpus evidence from 2+ sources; sequencing documented. Full content: clinical_rationale, timing_guidance, same_session_ok, patient_education_text, staff_talking_points. DB row with is_active=false until Chris approves.
- `common`: Widely practiced; 7-8 gates pass; moderate corpus evidence from 1+ source; useful for most patients. Full content. DB row with is_active=false until Chris approves.
- `conditional`: Works under specific conditions; 5-7 gates pass with explicit conditions noted. Content: clinical_rationale + timing_guidance + conditions (in source_reference). DB row, is_active=false.
- `compatible_only`: Technically safe to combine; no strong clinical rationale to actively recommend. Short rationale only. DB row, is_active=false.
- `do_not_market`: Relationship exists (especially alternatives per D-02) but should not be recommended as combination. Suppression rationale only. Selective DB row (per D-16).
- `rejected`: Fails safety gate OR "why both?" is empty. Report-only in PAIRING_EVALUATION.md, no DB row unless needed as hard suppression guardrail.

**## Gate Definitions**
For each of the 8 gates, document:
- Gate number and name
- Pass/fail criteria (free-text notes per gate, per D-04)
- Data sources (SQL-derivable or requires LLM/corpus)
- Hard stop status

Gates (per RESEARCH.md 8-gate table):
1. **Concern Overlap** — Do both products address at least one shared concern? Source: item_concerns JOIN. Not a hard stop (some valid pairs have no overlap — e.g., body contouring + neurotoxin for different areas).
2. **Mechanism Complementarity** — Do the products work through different mechanisms addressing different aspects of the concern? Source: dossier prose + does_not_solve + corpus. Pass if "Product A's limitation is addressed by Product B's mechanism."
3. **Limitation Coverage** — Does Product A's does_not_solve list include something Product B treats? Source: does_not_solve array cross-referenced with item_concerns. This is the core "why both?" gate.
4. **Timing Compatibility** — Can the products be used in the same treatment plan with safe sequencing? Source: product interval fields + corpus expert discussion. same_session_ok determination happens here.
5. **Safety** — No known contraindications, adverse interactions, or excessive dosing risk? Source: PubMed corpus, FDA labels, dossier clinical sections. **HARD STOP** — fail here means maximum tier is conditional (with explicit safety conditions) or do_not_market/rejected.
6. **Commercial Viability** — Would a practice realistically offer this combination? Source: podcast economics discussion, practice patterns. Eliminates obscure but technically valid combinations.
7. **Patient Clarity** — Can a patient understand why they need both treatments? Source: LLM judgment using aliases, patient_education patterns. Ensures the pairing is explainable, not just clinically valid.
8. **Source Support** — Is there corpus evidence (podcast expert discussion, PubMed study, clinical protocol) supporting this combination? Source: CMS corpus RPCs. Per no-corpus rule: zero evidence = maximum tier of conditional.

**## Hard Stops**
- Safety gate (Gate 5) fail -> cannot be canonical or common (per D-04)
- "Why both?" is promotional or circular -> rejected or do_not_market
- No corpus evidence -> cannot be canonical or common (per no-corpus rule)
- Staff talking points contain pressure language, guarantees, or unsupported claims -> fail review (per D-13)

**## Content-Depth Rules** (per D-12)
Table mapping tier to required fields:
| Tier | clinical_rationale | timing_guidance | same_session_ok | patient_education_text | staff_talking_points |
|------|---|---|---|---|---|
| canonical | Required | Required | Required | Required ("Tell the patient: ...") | Required (education-first hybrid) |
| common | Required | Required | Required | Required | Required |
| conditional | Required | Required + conditions | Required | Not required | Not required |
| compatible_only | Short rationale | Not required | Optional | Not required | Not required |
| do_not_market | Suppression rationale | Not required | N/A | Not required | Not required |
| rejected | Report-only | N/A | N/A | N/A | N/A |

**## Content Tone Rules**
- Per D-13: Staff talking points use hybrid clinical + consultation-ready tone. Education-first, not pressure-based. Gateway posture: clinical accuracy + patient education + consultation-ready phrasing. No pressure language, overpromising, guarantees, or unqualified claims. Good/better/best framing where appropriate.
- Per D-14: Patient education text uses provider-facing "Tell the patient: ..." framing. A360 is provider-mediated.
- Anti-pattern: "Upsell", "premium package", "guaranteed results", "you need both"
- Good pattern: "When a patient is focused on one concern, this helps the team explain why that treatment may only solve part of the issue..."

Include the 14-item checklist from CONTEXT.md verbatim.
